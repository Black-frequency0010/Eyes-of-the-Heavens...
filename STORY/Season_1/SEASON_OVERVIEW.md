# SEASON 1 OVERVIEW  
## TITLE: THE AWAKENING OF FINALITY

---

# 1. SEASON PURPOSE

Season 1 introduces the core world, the main characters, and the first discovery of a hidden system beneath reality.

This season is about:
- awakening into a hidden truth
- discovering that reality is governed by unseen rules
- the first encounter with “Finality” as a concept

The audience should feel:
> curiosity, mystery, and rising tension

---

# 2. CORE CONFLICT

The central conflict of Season 1 is:

> A normal reality is slowly revealing that it is being controlled by an unseen “finality system” that determines how events end.

At this stage:
- characters do NOT fully understand the system
- only anomalies and glitches are visible
- truth is fragmented and unclear

---

# 3. EPISODE FLOW STRUCTURE

Episode progression:

- EP 1–2 → Introduction of world + main characters  
- EP 3–5 → First anomalies appear in reality  
- EP 6–8 → Discovery of “Finality Signals”  
- EP 9–10 → First system-level interference is observed  
- EP 11–12 → Conflict escalation and hidden truth hints  
- EP 13 → Season climax + first major system reveal  

---

# 4. EMOTIONAL PROGRESSION

- Beginning → normal life / curiosity  
- Mid → confusion / mystery / unease  
- Late mid → fear / realization something is wrong  
- Ending → shock / revelation / hook into Season 2  

---

# 5. CHARACTER FOCUS SHIFT

Main focus progression:

- Early Season → Kairo (main perspective anchor)  
- Mid Season → Reika introduced as emotional balance + instability detection  
- Late Season → Kaze introduced as system-linked intelligence  
- End Season → first indirect mention of Mikaboshi  

Unknown Boy is NOT introduced yet.

---

# 6. LORE SYSTEM INTRODUCTION RULE

Season 1 introduces ONLY:

- basic reality structure  
- early “Finality anomalies”  
- weak system interference signs  

DO NOT fully explain:
- Witness system  
- Godform  
- Origin removal  
- Singularity protocols  

These are teased, not revealed.

---

# 7. CLIFFHANGER RULE

Every episode must end with at least ONE of:

- unexplained anomaly  
- contradiction in reality  
- system glitch behavior  
- missing causal explanation  

Season finale must end with:
> proof that reality is being actively controlled or edited

---

# 8. SEASON 1 ENDING PURPOSE

Season 1 ends not with resolution, but with:

> confirmation that reality is not natural — it is structured and controlled by an unknown system

This leads directly into Season 2 escalation.

---
