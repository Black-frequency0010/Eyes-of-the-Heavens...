EPISODE 2: THE WORLD LEARNS TO LIE

---

SCENE 1 — GLOBAL NEWS DISTORTION

The footage was everywhere.

Or at least, it had been for thirty-seven seconds.

A black fracture across the sky.

Captured from multiple cities.

Then deleted.

Then replaced.

Every news channel displayed the same statement:

«“Atmospheric imaging distortion caused by electromagnetic interference.”»

A lie.

And everyone knew it.

But people still wanted to believe it.

Because believing was easier than understanding the alternative.

In Aetherion City, screens flickered between reality and denial.

A news anchor smiled too hard.

Her eyes never blinked.

“This phenomenon poses no threat to public safety—”

The broadcast cut mid-sentence.

JOAD suppression protocol activated.

---

SCENE 2 — JOAD EMERGENCY COUNCIL

Deep underground.

Beneath government architecture that the public was never meant to see.

A circular chamber filled with holographic sky projections.

Dozens of officials stood frozen.

All watching the same anomaly replay.

The sky fracture.

Again.

And again.

A JOAD Director finally spoke.

“…This is not atmospheric.”

No one responded.

He continued.

“…We are witnessing a seal failure event.”

Silence became heavier.

Another officer spoke.

“Seal failure of what classification?”

No one wanted to answer that question.

Finally—

A lower-ranked analyst whispered:

“…Celestial-Class containment systems.”

The room went completely still.

Then alarms triggered automatically.

Not because someone pressed a button—

but because the system itself had recognized the truth.

Something sealed in the world had begun to wake.

---

SCENE 3 — TENSHIRŌ ESTATE BRIEFING

The atmosphere in the Tenshirō Estate had changed.

No longer peaceful.

No longer stable.

Kaze Tenshirō stood in the central chamber.

His presence alone made the air feel heavier.

Kairo stood to one side.

Reika beside him.

Haruto, Raion, Akari, Sora—aligned in silence.

Kaze spoke without raising his voice.

“The sky fracture was not a vision error.”

That sentence alone removed all doubt.

Reika’s fingers tightened slightly.

Kairo remained still.

Kaze continued.

“The seal is reacting to internal resonance.”

Akari spoke carefully.

“…Internal resonance?”

Kaze’s gaze shifted upward.

“To you.”

The room froze.

Reika did not move.

But something inside her reacted violently.

A faint pulse in her left eye.

Like something smiling in the dark.

---

SCENE 4 — PUBLIC COLLAPSE EVENT

Above the city—

the sky cracked again.

This time, it did not disappear.

It remained visible for twelve seconds.

Twelve seconds was enough.

Phones recorded it.

People screamed.

Traffic froze.

Some dropped to their knees.

Others laughed.

Because their minds refused to accept it.

A second fracture formed beside the first.

Then both vanished.

But the damage was done.

Reality had been proven unstable.

And the world could never unsee it again.

---

SCENE 5 — REIKA ALONE

That night.

Reika stood in her room alone.

The lights were off.

The estate was silent.

Except for one thing.

Her heartbeat.

Slow.

Measured.

Too aware.

Then—

the voice returned.

Closer than before.

Not inside her mind.

Inside her existence.

«“They are beginning to see.”»

Reika’s breath stopped.

She whispered into the darkness.

“…What are you?”

A pause.

Then—

«“I am what they buried.”»

Her eye burned.

Painful.

Beautiful.

Terrifying.

«“And you… are the first door that remembered how to open.”»

Reika stepped back instinctively.

Her reflection in the mirror shifted.

For a fraction of a second—

it did not copy her movement.

It smiled first.

Then the glass returned to normal.

And somewhere far beyond the sky—

something slowly turned its attention toward her completely.
