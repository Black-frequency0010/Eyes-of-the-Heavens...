EPISODE 4: WHEN CONTROL FAILS

---

SCENE 1 — JOAD LOSES THE BOARD

JOAD Headquarters was never supposed to panic.

That was the rule.

Control was the illusion they sold to the world.

But today—

control was gone.

Every screen in the command center flickered red.

Not error red.

Not warning red.

But something deeper.

System failure red.

A senior director stood frozen.

“…All containment nodes are offline.”

Silence.

Then another voice.

“That’s impossible.”

A third screen exploded into static.

Then reappeared with a symbol.

The broken eye.

Again.

And again.

Spreading across every feed like infection.

One analyst whispered:

“…It’s not hacking.”

The director turned sharply.

“What did you say?”

The analyst swallowed.

“It’s rewriting.”

---

SCENE 2 — VEILBREAKERS REVEAL THEMSELVES

Above ground, the city was collapsing into confusion.

But the Veilbreakers were already visible.

For the first time.

Not hidden.

Not underground.

Standing across fractured rooftops like they had always belonged there.

Zenith looked down at Aetherion City.

Calm.

Almost disappointed.

“They still believe they are safe.”

A follower spoke.

“Should we initiate full rupture?”

Zenith shook his head slightly.

“No.”

A pause.

Then—

“Not rupture.”

His eyes narrowed.

“Awakening.”

Below them—

the sky cracked.

And stayed cracked.

For the first time.

---

SCENE 3 — TENSHIRŌ EMERGENCY RESPONSE

Kaze Tenshirō stood alone in the central chamber.

The clan behind him was silent.

Even Raion did not speak.

Haruto’s hand rested on his blade.

Akari’s eyes trembled with unstable vision flashes.

Sora looked like he had already read the end of the story.

Kaze finally spoke.

“The seal is no longer stable.”

Reika’s breath tightened.

Kairo stepped forward.

“What changed?”

Kaze’s expression darkened slightly.

“…It responded.”

Silence.

Akari whispered:

“To what?”

Kaze looked at Reika.

Not Kairo.

Reika.

“To her.”

Reika froze.

Her left eye pulsed violently.

Painful.

Awakening.

Something inside her smiled again.

---

SCENE 4 — FIRST OUTSIDE CONTACT

A JOAD convoy disappeared mid-transit.

No explosion.

No signal.

Just silence.

Then—

a message appeared on every surviving device in the region.

A single line:

«“Containment is a delay, not a solution.”»

Then the systems shut down permanently.

Across the world, governments finally admitted something publicly:

They had lost control.

Panic began.

Not small panic.

Civil panic.

Systemic panic.

The kind that does not stop.

---

SCENE 5 — REIKA BREAK POINT

That night.

Reika could not sleep.

The sky outside her window flickered every few seconds.

Like something was blinking behind reality.

She stepped outside.

Alone.

The wind felt wrong.

Heavier.

Older.

Then—

the voice returned.

Closer than ever.

«“They are afraid of you.”»

Reika whispered:

“…I didn’t do anything.”

A pause.

Then the voice responded:

«“Not yet.”»

The sky above her split open briefly.

Not fully.

Just enough to reveal something behind it.

Not darkness.

Not light.

Something watching.

Reika took a step back.

Her Seraphic Eye activated on its own.

For the first time—

she did not control it.

And for a moment—

reality bent around her fear.
