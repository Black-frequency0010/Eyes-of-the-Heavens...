EPISODE 3: THE FIRST REAL BREAK

---

SCENE 1 — QUIET BEFORE THE ERROR

The world pretended nothing was wrong.

That was its final defense mechanism.

In Aetherion City, people returned to routine faster than expected.

News channels had already changed the narrative.

Social media posts were flagged, deleted, rewritten.

Reality was being edited in real time.

JOAD called it “global stabilization protocol.”

But inside the Tenshirō Estate—

no one believed that lie anymore.

Kairo stood in the training courtyard alone.

His Heavenly Eye remained sealed.

But his presence already bent the air around him.

Haruto watched from a distance.

Waiting.

Observing.

Then—

the wind stopped.

Completely.

Kairo looked up slowly.

“…It’s starting again.”

---

SCENE 2 — VEILBREAKERS MOVE

Far beneath the city.

Inside a fractured underground temple that should not exist on any map.

The Veilbreakers gathered.

Zenith stood at the center.

Calm.

Almost peaceful.

Behind him, fractured symbols of ancient seals flickered like dying stars.

A subordinate spoke nervously.

“The JOAD has increased suppression efforts.”

Zenith smiled slightly.

“That means we are close.”

Another voice.

“To what end?”

Zenith turned slowly.

His eyes carried something deeper than hatred.

Something closer to disappointment.

“To the moment they stop lying.”

Silence.

Then he raised his hand.

And the seal wall behind him cracked open.

Not violently.

Elegantly.

Like reality agreeing to be corrected.

---

SCENE 3 — FIRST OUTSIDE BREACH

A JOAD monitoring station exploded without warning.

No attack.

No signal.

No energy trace.

Just disappearance.

Within seconds, twelve more stations followed.

Every screen inside JOAD headquarters turned black.

Then displayed a single symbol.

A broken eye.

Alert systems screamed.

But no one could shut them down.

One analyst whispered:

“…They’re inside the network.”

A director slammed his fist.

“Inside where?!”

No one answered.

Because the truth was worse than infiltration.

It was recognition.

Something had learned how their systems worked.

---

SCENE 4 — TENSHIRŌ RESPONSE

Kaze stood before the clan council.

His expression had not changed.

But the weight in the room had doubled.

“The Veilbreakers have initiated partial unsealing operations.”

Akari stiffened.

“…Partial?”

Kaze nodded.

“Something is responding to them.”

Reika felt it immediately.

A pulse in her left eye.

Not pain.

Not activation.

Awareness.

Like something far away had noticed her noticing it.

Kairo stepped forward.

“We intercept?”

Kaze paused.

A rare silence.

Then—

“No.”

Everyone turned.

Kaze’s voice lowered.

“Not yet.”

That single sentence carried more danger than any order.

---

SCENE 5 — REIKA’S FRACTURE MOMENT

That night.

Reika stood alone again.

The mirror reflected her perfectly.

Until it didn’t.

For half a second—

her reflection tilted its head first.

Not her.

Something wearing her shape.

Reika stepped back.

Her breathing slowed.

“…Stop.”

The reflection smiled.

Not kindly.

Not cruelly.

Something else.

Understanding.

Then—

a voice behind her.

Soft.

Patient.

«“You are resisting less than before.”»

Reika turned sharply.

Nothing there.

But her eye burned.

A single black line appeared in her vision.

Like a crack forming across reality itself.

She whispered:

“…What do you want from me?”

The answer came instantly.

«“Only what you already are becoming.”»

Her Seraphic Eye flared.

The room distorted.

For one moment—

the entire estate outside the window appeared broken.

Like glass layered over existence.

Then it vanished.

Reika collapsed to one knee.

Breathing uneven.

For the first time—

she was afraid of her own power.

---

SCENE 6 — THE SKY REMEMBERS

Above Aetherion City.

The sky cracked again.

But this time—

it did not disappear immediately.

It stayed.

For exactly 3.7 seconds.

Long enough for millions to see it.

Long enough for fear to become permanent.

Long enough for reality to lose authority.

And somewhere beyond that fracture—

something opened its eyes completely.
