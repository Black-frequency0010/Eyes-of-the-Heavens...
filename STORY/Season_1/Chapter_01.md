EYES OF THE HEAVENS: TRINITY ASCENSION

Season 1 — First Alignment

Episode 1: The Twins Beneath Heaven (Reforged Hook Version)

---

PROLOGUE OF SILENCE

The sky was too clean.

That was always the first sign.

Aetherion City woke beneath a perfect blue horizon, untouched by storms, untouched by war, untouched by anything that should remind humanity it was fragile.

Light spread across the glass towers like water spilling over invisible stone.

The world looked stable.

That illusion was the most dangerous thing in existence.

Because somewhere beyond that sky—

reality was already breaking.

---

SCENE 1 — THE BALCONY OF QUIET GODS

Reika Tenshirō stood alone on the eastern balcony of the estate.

Wind moved through her silver hair like a living thing.

Below her, the world continued as if nothing in existence was wrong.

Cars moved.

Voices echoed.

Machines obeyed.

Life behaved normally.

But Reika did not.

Her Seraphic Eye was quiet today.

Too quiet.

That was worse than pain.

“…Again.”

Her voice barely existed in the air.

She pressed her fingers lightly against her left eye.

A faint pulse answered her.

Not hers.

Something else’s.

A presence that did not belong inside her body.

She exhaled slowly.

And then—

a voice answered back.

Not in the air.

Not in her ears.

Inside her perception.

«“You keep looking upward, little Seraph.”»

Reika froze.

Her breath stopped completely.

The sky did not move.

The world did not react.

No one heard it.

Except her.

---

SCENE 2 — THE ONLY PERSON WHO ANNOYS HER

“You’re doing it again.”

Reika didn’t turn immediately.

She already knew who it was.

“Kairo.”

He stepped beside her like the world belonged to him by default.

Calm.

Measured.

Too calm.

His Heavenly Eye remained closed, but its presence was already heavy enough to distort the atmosphere around him.

He followed her gaze upward.

“…You’ve been doing this every morning.”

Reika forced a smile.

“And you’ve been counting again.”

“I notice patterns.”

“That’s called obsession.”

“That’s called survival.”

Silence.

Then—

Reika sighed.

“Do you ever feel like something is watching us?”

Kairo did not answer immediately.

That alone was rare.

Then—

“Yes.”

A single word.

He didn’t elaborate.

He didn’t joke.

He didn’t soften it.

That made it worse.

---

SCENE 3 — BREAKFAST OF UNWILLING GODS

The estate dining hall felt like a cathedral pretending to be a home.

Haruto stood near the entrance.

Silent.

Unmoving.

Raion was already eating like nothing in the world could kill him.

Akari sat carefully, her fingers trembling slightly around her cup.

Sora stared into nothing.

As if remembering things no one else could see.

Kairo and Reika entered.

The room subtly shifted.

Not physically.

Emotionally.

Like gravity changed direction.

Kairo sat first.

Reika followed.

No one spoke for a moment.

Then—

Akari dropped her cup.

It shattered.

Everyone turned instantly.

Her pupils shimmered faint gold.

Oracle Eye activation.

Uncontrolled.

“I saw it.”

Her voice cracked.

Something in the room tightened.

Kairo didn’t move.

“Say it clearly.”

Akari shook.

“I don’t know what it is…”

Her voice lowered.

“But it saw me back.”

Silence.

That sentence landed too deeply.

Because in their world—

nothing was supposed to see an Oracle Eye.

Nothing.

---

SCENE 4 — THE FIRST FRACTURE

The air changed.

Not metaphorically.

Physically.

The light in the room flickered once.

Just once.

Reika’s hand stopped mid-motion.

She felt it again.

That presence.

Closer now.

Not outside.

Not far away.

But leaning into reality itself.

And then—

the sky outside the window cracked.

Not loudly.

Not violently.

Like glass remembering it was never meant to hold the sky.

A thin black fracture spread across heaven.

One second.

Then gone.

But it was enough.

Kairo was already standing.

Haruto drew breath sharply.

Raion stopped moving.

Sora whispered something under his breath:

“…So it has begun.”

Reika did not speak.

Because inside her mind—

the voice returned.

Calm.

Patient.

Almost gentle.

«“Reika Tenshirō…”»

Her heart stopped.

The voice continued.

«“You are finally awake.”»
