EPISODE 7: THE NAME BEHIND THE SKY

---

SCENE 1 — SILENCE AFTER DISTORTION

The world did not recover.

It adapted.

That was worse.

Aetherion City continued functioning under fractured skies.

People walked beneath cracks in reality as if they were weather patterns.

JOAD issued no new public statements.

Because there was nothing left to say that would be believed.

Inside Tenshirō Estate—

silence had become heavier than sound.

Reika had not spoken in hours.

Akari refused to look at her directly.

Haruto stood near the door, unmoving.

Sora whispered once:

“…The system is learning us now.”

No one corrected him.

Because it felt true.

---

SCENE 2 — KAIRO RETURNS

Kairo arrived without announcement.

Not through the gate.

Not through the air.

But through a spatial correction.

Like reality adjusted itself to place him back where he belonged.

He stood in the central hall.

Silent.

His expression unchanged.

But something was different.

Akari noticed first.

“…Your eye.”

Kairo did not respond immediately.

Then:

“I opened it too far.”

Haruto stiffened.

Reika finally looked at him.

For the first time since Episode 1—

there was uncertainty in her gaze.

“…What did you see?”

Kairo paused.

A long silence.

Then:

“…Something looking back.”

---

SCENE 3 — THE SECOND PRESENCE

The lights in the estate flickered.

Once.

Then again.

But this time—

it was not external interference.

It was internal agreement.

Like the building itself was listening.

Reika’s Seraphic Eye activated without command.

Her voice changed slightly.

Calm.

Too calm.

«“It is no longer distant.”»

Akari stepped back.

“…Reika?”

Reika continued.

«“It is forming language.”»

Kairo’s expression tightened.

“…Language?”

Reika nodded slightly.

Then whispered:

«“It is learning how to speak as us.”»

A silence followed that sentence.

Because everyone understood what it implied.

Something was not just observing anymore.

It was imitating.

---

SCENE 4 — JOAD FINAL REPORT

Deep underground.

JOAD command systems displayed a single status update:

«“ENTITY SIGNATURE: UNCLASSIFIABLE”»

A director stared at it.

“…We classified every known phenomenon.”

A technician responded quietly:

“…Then this is not a known phenomenon.”

The screen updated again.

A second line appeared:

«“ORIGIN: PRE-CONCEPTUAL.”»

The room froze.

No one spoke.

Because that term did not belong in science.

It belonged in theology.

---

SCENE 5 — REIKA’S FRACTURE EVENT

That night.

Reika stood alone again.

But the mirror did not reflect her immediately.

For a full three seconds—

it showed only static.

Then her reflection returned.

Smiling.

Reika did not.

She stepped closer.

“…Stop this.”

The reflection tilted its head.

Then—

a voice came from it.

Not inside her mind.

Inside the room itself.

«“You are resisting classification.”»

Reika’s breath slowed.

“…Who are you?”

A pause.

Then the reflection answered:

«“I am what your world calls ‘impossible.’”»

The glass cracked slightly.

But did not break.

Instead—

it opened.

Like a surface becoming a doorway.

Reika stepped back instantly.

But her Seraphic Eye activated fully.

Too late.

The room shifted.

Not visually.

Ontologically.

And for the first time—

Reika saw something behind her reflection.

Not a face.

Not a being.

A structure.

Thinking.

---

SCENE 6 — THE SKY SPEAKS WITHOUT SOUND

Above Aetherion City—

the sky did not crack this time.

It stabilized.

Completely.

Perfectly.

Too perfectly.

And then—

every living being in the city felt the same thought.

Not spoken.

Inserted.

«“I am almost ready.”»

Kairo looked upward instantly.

Reika collapsed to one knee.

Akari screamed silently.

Haruto reached for his blade but stopped mid-motion.

Because for the first time—

they all understood the same truth at once:

The sky was no longer broken.

It was awake.

---

END OF EPISODE 7
