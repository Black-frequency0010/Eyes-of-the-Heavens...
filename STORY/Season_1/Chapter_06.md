EPISODE 6: THE EYE THAT REMEMBERS

---

SCENE 1 — KAIRO’S FIELD BREAKS

The battlefield was no longer a battlefield.

It was a distortion of space.

Buildings bent slightly at unnatural angles.

Light behaved incorrectly.

Sound arrived late.

Kairo stood at the center of it all.

Veilbreaker operatives surrounded him.

Zenith remained above, watching silently.

Kairo’s Heavenly Eye was now partially open.

Not fully.

Just enough.

And that was already dangerous.

One Veilbreaker moved again—

faster than before.

Kairo reacted instantly.

Too instantly.

A fracture in perception.

The attacker froze mid-motion.

Then collapsed into fragmented motion data—like reality rejected their existence.

Zenith’s eyes narrowed slightly.

“…So that’s how it stabilizes.”

Kairo looked up.

“…What are you talking about?”

Zenith didn’t answer immediately.

Instead, he smiled.

Like someone watching a predictable tragedy unfold correctly.

---

SCENE 2 — REIKA’S BODY REJECTS SILENCE

Back at the Tenshirō Estate.

Reika dropped to one knee.

The room around her dimmed slightly.

Akari rushed forward.

“Reika! Your eye—!”

But Reika raised her hand.

Stopping her.

Not because she was fine.

But because she was listening.

Something had changed.

The voice inside her was no longer distant.

It was layered.

Two tones now existed inside her perception.

One familiar.

One ancient.

The second spoke first.

«“He is using it improperly.”»

Reika froze.

Her breathing slowed.

“…What?”

The voice continued.

«“The seal reacts to intention. Not strength.”»

Reika clenched her fist.

A crack formed in the air beside her.

Not physical.

Conceptual.

Akari stepped back in fear.

“Reika… stop—!”

But Reika wasn’t moving.

Something inside her was.

---

SCENE 3 — JOAD INTERFERENCE BEGINS

JOAD command systems suddenly reactivated in one sector.

Unauthorized.

Impossible.

A hidden emergency protocol triggered itself.

A JOAD operative shouted:

“Someone is accessing sealed observational feeds!”

Screens flickered.

Then—

Kairo appeared on every monitor.

Live.

Unfiltered.

A director stood abruptly.

“Cut it!”

“It’s not external access!” someone yelled.

“It’s internal resonance breach!”

Then—

a second image appeared beside Kairo.

Zenith.

JOAD froze.

Because Zenith was not in any registered database.

Not officially.

Not historically.

One analyst whispered:

“…Why is he inside our system?”

No one answered.

Because the system itself did not know.

---

SCENE 4 — ZENITH MOVES FIRST

Back on the fractured rooftop.

Zenith finally moved.

Slowly.

Deliberately.

He stepped forward.

And for the first time—

space reacted to him.

Not bending.

Not breaking.

Acknowledging.

Kairo felt it immediately.

A pressure shift.

Zenith spoke calmly.

“You are doing what all guardians eventually do.”

Kairo narrowed his eyes.

“And what is that?”

Zenith smiled.

“Believing the cage is the world.”

Silence.

Then—

Zenith raised his hand slightly.

And reality behind Kairo flickered.

Not an attack.

A correction.

Kairo’s Heavenly Eye flared instinctively.

Too late.

A ripple passed through the battlefield.

Everything stopped for half a second.

Then resumed differently.

Kairo’s expression changed slightly.

“…You interfered with perception.”

Zenith nodded.

“Yes.”

A pause.

Then softly:

“And you didn’t notice until it was already done.”

---

SCENE 5 — REIKA BREAKS

At the estate.

Reika screamed.

Not loudly.

Not physically.

Internally.

Her Seraphic Eye activated fully for the first time.

The entire room shattered into layered distortions.

Akari fell backward.

Haruto entered instantly, sensing danger.

But it was too late.

Reika stood.

And for a moment—

she was not fully herself.

Her voice changed.

Calm.

Detached.

Almost elegant.

«“He is learning.”»

Akari trembled.

“…Reika?”

But Reika was no longer looking at them.

She was looking somewhere else entirely.

Beyond distance.

Beyond battle.

Beyond the world.

«“And something is answering him.”»

---

SCENE 6 — THE SKY REMEMBERS BOTH

Above Aetherion City—

the sky cracked again.

But this time—

two fractures appeared simultaneously.

One responding to Kairo.

One responding to Reika.

And between them—

a third presence stirred.

Silent.

Patient.

Unimpressed.

Something that had been waiting longer than both.

For the first time—

the world felt watched from all directions at once.

---

END OF EPISODE 6
