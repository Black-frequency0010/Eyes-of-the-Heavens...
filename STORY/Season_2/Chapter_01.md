EPISODE 1: STARFALL BEGINNING

---

SCENE 1 — THE WORLD AFTER THE WORD

The phrase remained.

«“THIS IS NOT AN END. THIS IS A FIRST ITERATION.”»

But nothing followed it.

No destruction.

No salvation.

Only continuation.

The world did not reset.

It adapted incorrectly.

Aetherion City now existed in what scholars would later (incorrectly) call:

«“Post-Interpretation Reality”»

But at the time—

no one had language for it.

---

SCENE 2 — KAIRO’S ISOLATION

Kairo stood alone on the outer observation platform of the Tenshirō estate.

The sky was stable again.

Too stable.

Like it had learned how to pretend.

Haruto approached behind him.

“…We lost contact with JOAD completely.”

Kairo didn’t turn.

“That was expected.”

A pause.

Haruto hesitated.

“…Expected?”

Kairo finally spoke:

“It was never a system meant to survive contact with intelligence beyond classification.”

Silence.

Then quietly:

“…We are on our own now.”

---

SCENE 3 — REIKA’S DISTANCE

Reika had changed.

Not visibly.

But structurally.

She moved like someone sharing space with another presence.

Akari noticed immediately.

“…Reika, are you still… you?”

Reika paused.

Then answered gently:

“I don’t think that question works anymore.”

Akari froze.

Reika continued:

“Because ‘me’ is no longer a single thing.”

---

SCENE 4 — FIRST SKY EVENT (STARFALL PHENOMENON)

At exactly 03:17 AM—

the sky broke in silence again.

But this time—

instead of a voice or symbol,

something fell.

Not meteors.

Not light.

But fragments of structured memory.

They did not burn.

They landed.

And where they landed—

people began remembering things they never experienced.

Entire emotional histories that did not belong to them.

The world called it:

«“Starfall Incident 01”»

JOAD was no longer there to classify it.

So humanity classified itself instead.

Poorly.

---

SCENE 5 — VEILBREAKERS OBSERVE FIRST REAL CHANGE

Zenith stood beneath the falling fragments.

One landed near him.

He touched it.

Paused.

Then smiled faintly.

“…So it begins.”

A follower asked:

“What begins?”

Zenith looked upward.

“The world is no longer receiving information from reality.”

A pause.

“It is receiving memory from something else.”

---

SCENE 6 — THE TENSHIRŌ CLAN RESPONSE

Emergency meeting.

Kaze stood at the center.

Haruto, Raion, Akari, Sora, Kairo, Reika.

All present.

Kaze spoke:

“This is no longer containment.”

A pause.

“This is assimilation.”

Raion clenched his fist.

“Then we fight it.”

Sora shook his head slightly.

“…You can’t fight memory.”

Akari whispered:

“Then what do we do?”

Kaze looked at Kairo.

Kairo answered quietly:

“…We learn what is sending it.”

Reika added softly:

“…Or it learns us first.”

Silence.

No one disagreed.

Because both outcomes were already happening.

---

SCENE 7 — FIRST DIRECT IMPACT ON HUMANS

Across the city—

people began collapsing.

Not from injury.

From overload.

They were remembering lives that were not theirs.

Some cried.

Some laughed.

Some simply stared into space, repeating words like:

“I remember dying in a place I have never been.”

Hospitals filled.

Not with patients.

With conflicting existences.

---

SCENE 8 — FINAL MOMENT OF EPISODE

Kairo stood alone again.

Above him—

a fragment of Starfall hovered.

Not moving.

Waiting.

Then—

it spoke without sound.

Not through voice.

Through understanding.

«“WE ARE NOT GIVING YOU MEMORY.”»

A pause.

«“WE ARE RETURNING IT.”»

Kairo’s expression tightened.

“…Return from where?”

The fragment responded:

«“FROM WHAT YOU HAVE ALWAYS BEEN INSIDE.”»

The fragment dissolved.

And Kairo realized something terrifying:

This was not invasion.

This was correction.

---

END OF EPISODE 1 — STARFALL BEGINNING
