SEASON 3

EPISODE 2: THE OCEAN BELOW EXISTENCE

---

SCENE 1 — THE DREAM THAT DOESN'T END

Most dreams disappear when people wake.

This one remained.

Across the world, millions remembered every detail.

The black ocean.

The stars.

The eye beneath the water.

The voice.

For the first time since Starfall Alignment—

humanity experienced something together that the system could not immediately standardize.

People painted it.

Wrote about it.

Recorded it.

And every version matched.

Perfectly.

That had never happened before.

---

SCENE 2 — REIKA ADMITS THE TRUTH

Inside the Tenshirō estate.

Kairo and Reika stood alone.

Morning sunlight entered through the windows.

Neither spoke for several moments.

Finally:

"Kairo."

He looked up.

Reika's voice was quiet.

Dangerously quiet.

"...I can hear it now."

Silence.

Kairo already knew what she meant.

"The Observer?"

Reika slowly shook her head.

"No."

A pause.

"The Observer is scared of it too."

For the first time in years—

Kairo genuinely felt fear.

---

SCENE 3 — YUMI'S DISCOVERY

Far away.

Inside a hidden research facility.

Dr. Yumi Kagami stared at data she could not explain.

Every alignment measurement on Earth had changed.

Not broken.

Changed direction.

Like a compass suddenly recognizing a new north.

She enlarged the projection.

Thousands of lines moved toward a single point.

One impossible location.

A place that technically did not exist.

Yet every system insisted it was there.

Yumi whispered:

"...Something is attracting reality."

---

SCENE 4 — THE FIRST CRACK IN THE SYSTEM

For years, Starfall had been increasing order.

Now—

small contradictions began returning.

A man remembered two childhoods.

A woman recalled conversations she never had.

Entire neighborhoods reported brief flashes of alternate events.

The system attempted correction.

And failed.

That had never happened before.

---

SCENE 5 — ZENITH'S CONFESSION

The Veilbreakers gathered.

Expecting answers.

Instead—

Zenith gave them a confession.

"I was wrong."

Silence.

Nobody spoke.

Nobody moved.

Because Zenith never admitted error.

Ever.

A follower finally asked:

"Wrong about what?"

Zenith looked exhausted.

Older.

For the first time in decades.

"I thought Mikaboshi wanted freedom."

A pause.

"I thought he wanted release."

The room remained silent.

Zenith continued:

"...I no longer think those words mean anything to him."

---

SCENE 6 — KAZE OPENS THE FORBIDDEN RECORD

Deep beneath the Tenshirō estate.

A chamber sealed for centuries.

A room even Kairo had never entered.

Kaze unlocked it personally.

Inside stood a single object.

A stone archive older than the clan itself.

Its surface contained a warning.

Not instructions.

Not history.

A warning.

It read:

«IF THE OCEAN IS SEEN,

THE CONTAINMENT HAS ALREADY FAILED.»

Kaze stared at the inscription.

Then continued reading.

The second line was worse.

Much worse.

«THE FALLEN STAR IS NOT INSIDE THE PRISON.

THE PRISON IS INSIDE THE FALLEN STAR.»

---

SCENE 7 — THE HORRIBLE POSSIBILITY

That evening.

The core group gathered.

Kairo.

Reika.

Kaze.

Haruto.

Akari.

Raion.

Sora.

Yumi via transmission.

Kaze revealed the inscription.

Nobody spoke immediately.

Because nobody wanted to say the obvious conclusion.

Eventually Akari did.

Voice trembling.

"...If that's true..."

Silence.

"...then we've misunderstood everything."

Yumi finished the thought.

"The seals."

Haruto looked up.

"The clan."

Raion frowned.

"Starfall."

Sora whispered:

"Silent Heaven."

Then Kairo spoke.

Slowly.

Carefully.

Like someone stepping toward an abyss.

"...All of it may have been inside something much larger."

---

SCENE 8 — FINAL MOMENT

That night.

The black ocean appeared again.

Not in dreams.

In the sky.

For exactly three seconds.

Every human being on Earth saw it.

A vast dark sea replacing the heavens.

Stars reflected on impossible water.

And beneath it—

movement.

Massive.

Ancient.

Patient.

The vision vanished immediately.

Governments denied it.

Scientists couldn't explain it.

The system couldn't suppress it.

And deep inside her Seraphic Eye—

Reika heard the voice again.

Closer this time.

Gentler.

Almost compassionate.

«"Little star."»

A pause.

«"You are beginning to remember."»

Reika froze.

Because she had never heard that voice before.

Yet somehow—

it sounded familiar.

CUT TO BLACK.
