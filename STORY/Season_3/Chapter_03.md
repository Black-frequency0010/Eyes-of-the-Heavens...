SEASON 3

EPISODE 3: THE MEMORY THAT ISN'T YOURS

---

SCENE 1 — REIKA REMEMBERS SOMETHING IMPOSSIBLE

Three days after the Black Ocean appeared in the sky.

Reika stopped sleeping.

Not because she couldn't.

Because every time she closed her eyes—

she remembered things that had never happened.

At least, they had never happened to her.

A city made entirely of light.

A sky with seven moons.

A child standing at the edge of a sea of stars.

A voice asking:

«"If destiny cannot change, why create choice?"»

Then destruction.

Silence.

Darkness.

Every memory felt real.

Every emotion belonged to someone else.

And yet—

she felt them inside her own heart.

---

SCENE 2 — SORA'S HORRIBLE DISCOVERY

Sora activated the Memoria Eye.

For decades, he had carried the memories of generations.

Thousands of lives.

Centuries of history.

The weight was unbearable.

But familiar.

This was different.

Hidden beneath the Tenshirō bloodline archive—

he found memories that did not belong to any human being.

Not Kaze.

Not Haruka.

Not any ancestor.

Something else.

Something older.

The moment he touched them—

he screamed.

Blood poured from both eyes.

Haruto caught him before he collapsed.

"What did you see?!"

Sora could barely breathe.

"...A funeral."

Silence.

"...The funeral of a universe."

---

SCENE 3 — JOAD BEGINS TO PANIC

For years, JOAD controlled information.

Contained incidents.

Managed public awareness.

Now they were losing.

Fast.

Reports flooded in.

Children speaking unknown languages.

People drawing celestial maps that matched no known stars.

Millions reporting identical visions.

Entire communities experiencing synchronized memories.

One director whispered:

"We're past containment."

Another answered:

"No."

A pause.

"We're past understanding."

---

SCENE 4 — YUMI BREAKS THE DATA WALL

Inside her research facility.

Yumi finally found a pattern.

The impossible memories.

The dreams.

The ocean.

The visual anomalies.

Everything pointed toward one conclusion.

She stared at the screen.

Then laughed.

Not because it was funny.

Because it was absurd.

"...Of course."

A pause.

"...They're not memories."

Silence.

Then:

"They're echoes."

---

SCENE 5 — WHAT AN ECHO REALLY IS

The team gathered.

Yumi explained.

No one liked what they heard.

"Memories belong to the past."

She pointed at the projection.

"These don't."

Akari frowned.

"What does that mean?"

Yumi took a breath.

"It means people aren't remembering something that happened."

Silence.

Then:

"They're remembering something that still exists."

Nobody spoke.

Because everyone understood how impossible that statement was.

---

SCENE 6 — KAIRO'S EYE EVOLVES

That night.

Kairo activated the Heavenly Eye again.

The backlash was immediate.

Blood.

Pain.

Distortion.

But this time—

he pushed through.

And finally saw the structure beneath reality.

Not just the system.

Not just Starfall.

Something deeper.

Something hidden underneath every layer.

A network.

A framework.

A prison.

No.

Not a prison.

A wound.

Kairo staggered backward.

"...Dear God."

Then he corrected himself.

"...No."

Because whatever he had seen—

it was older than gods.

---

SCENE 7 — REIKA HEARS HER NAME

Alone in the central chamber.

Reika stood in silence.

The voice returned.

Closer than ever.

Not speaking through power.

Not speaking through corruption.

Simply speaking.

«"Reika."»

She froze.

Because this time—

it used her name.

Not little star.

Not vessel.

Not interface.

Reika.

The voice continued.

«"Why are you afraid of me?"»

Her hands trembled.

"...Because everyone says I should be."

A pause.

Then soft laughter.

Not cruel.

Not mocking.

Almost sad.

«"That is a very human answer."»

---

SCENE 8 — FINAL MOMENT

Across the world—

people began seeing them.

Not the ocean.

Not the eye.

Figures.

Standing at impossible distances.

On rooftops.

At shorelines.

In empty streets.

Watching.

Silent.

Motionless.

Every witness described them the same way.

Humanoid.

Dark.

Featureless.

And carrying fragments of starlight beneath their skin.

The first global report appeared thirty minutes later.

Then ten thousand more followed.

Governments issued alerts.

JOAD declared emergency status.

Silent Heaven activated additional containment layers.

And deep beneath reality—

something shifted.

Not awakening.

Not moving.

Recognizing.

As though it had finally become aware that humanity could now see the edges of its shadow.

CUT TO BLACK.
