SEASON 3

EPISODE 4: THE MEN WHO STOOD AT THE EDGE OF CREATION

---

SCENE 1 — THE FIGURES DO NOT MOVE

The figures appeared everywhere.

Tokyo.

London.

Lagos.

New York.

Moscow.

São Paulo.

Places separated by oceans and cultures.

Yet they all looked identical.

Tall.

Silent.

Humanoid.

Dark silhouettes filled with distant starlight.

The strangest part?

They never moved.

Not once.

Cameras recorded them for hours.

Days.

They simply stood there.

Watching.

Waiting.

---

SCENE 2 — FIRST CONTACT ATTEMPT

JOAD launched an operation.

Thirty elite containment agents.

Three ocular users.

Military support.

The target stood on a deserted coastline.

Perfectly still.

The agents approached.

Fifty meters.

Nothing.

Twenty meters.

Nothing.

Ten meters.

Still nothing.

Then one agent asked:

"Who are you?"

The figure slowly raised its head.

For the first time—

it moved.

And every electronic device within five kilometers immediately failed.

The cameras died.

Communications vanished.

Power grids collapsed.

The only surviving audio recording captured one sentence:

«"We are what remained."»

Then the transmission ended.

---

SCENE 3 — THE RETURN OF FEAR

Starfall Alignment had reduced fear.

Now fear returned.

Not through violence.

Through uncertainty.

Because humanity finally encountered something that did not fit.

Something the system could not classify.

Something reality itself seemed uncomfortable acknowledging.

For the first time in years—

people began asking questions again.

And questions are dangerous.

---

SCENE 4 — SORA'S SECOND MEMORY

Sora entered the archive chamber alone.

Against everyone's advice.

Against his own judgment.

He touched the hidden memories again.

Immediately—

the vision returned.

This time he saw more.

A civilization older than stars.

Not advanced.

Not primitive.

Something beyond both concepts.

He saw beings standing before an impossible horizon.

Watching reality being created.

Watching galaxies form.

Watching time itself begin.

And among them—

one figure turned.

Looked directly at him.

And whispered:

«"You are late."»

Sora woke up screaming.

---

SCENE 5 — ZENITH FINALLY SPEAKS THE NAME

The Veilbreakers gathered.

For years, they worshipped freedom.

For years, they sought Mikaboshi.

Now Zenith finally revealed something he had hidden from everyone.

Even from himself.

"The figures have a name."

Silence.

Every eye turned toward him.

Zenith continued:

"...In the oldest forbidden records..."

A pause.

"...they were called Witnesses."

Nobody spoke.

Because somehow—

that title felt worse than soldiers.

Worse than gods.

Worse than monsters.

Witnesses.

People who observe.

People who remember.

People who survive.

---

SCENE 6 — REIKA'S CONVERSATION

The voice returned.

This time Reika did not resist.

She sat alone beneath the moonlight.

And listened.

«"Do you know why humanity fears me?"»

Reika answered honestly.

"...Because you're powerful."

The voice responded immediately.

«"No."»

A pause.

«"Power only frightens people who believe they can lose."»

Silence.

Then:

«"Humanity fears me because I remember."»

Reika's breath caught.

The voice continued.

«"And memory is heavier than judgment."»

---

SCENE 7 — KAIRO DISCOVERS THE FIRST WITNESS

Late that night.

Kairo found one.

Standing at the edge of an abandoned district.

Silent.

Motionless.

Watching the city lights.

The Witness sensed him immediately.

Slowly—

it turned.

For the first time—

Kairo saw its face.

And froze.

Because the face wasn't unfamiliar.

It looked human.

Not identical.

Not exact.

But somehow—

it carried features from countless people.

As though humanity itself had been blended into a single expression.

The Witness spoke.

Its voice sounded like many voices layered together.

«"You carry the wound."»

Kairo activated the Heavenly Eye instantly.

"...What are you?"

The Witness looked at him.

And answered:

«"A survivor."»

---

SCENE 8 — FINAL MOMENT

Far beneath reality.

Far beneath the system.

Far beneath Silent Heaven.

Something shifted.

Not physically.

Emotionally.

As though an ancient consciousness had felt something unexpected.

Interest.

Across the planet—

every Witness raised its head at the exact same moment.

Millions of people saw it happen.

Every government recorded it.

Every sensor confirmed it.

Then—

all Witnesses spoke simultaneously.

One sentence.

One message.

One impossible declaration.

«"THE FIRST MEMORY HAS RETURNED."»

And somewhere beyond existence—

something opened another eye.

CUT TO BLACK.
